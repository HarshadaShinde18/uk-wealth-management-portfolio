## Project Overview

This project simulates the role of a **UK-based wealth manager** tasked with designing,
constructing, and evaluating a **Â£2 million investment portfolio** for a private client.

The portfolio is developed in line with a structured **Investment Policy Statement (IPS)** and
includes:
- UK equities listed on the London Stock Exchange (LSE)
- A UK market benchmark (FTSE Index)
- British Government securities (UK Gilts) used as a proxy for the risk-free rate

Portfolio performance is analysed over a two-year period from  
**1 January 2021 to 31 December 2022**, incorporating both **risk and return characteristics**
and relevant **economic and market events** during the period.

---

## Project Objectives

The key objectives of this project are to:

- Develop a comprehensive **Investment Policy Statement (IPS)** aligned with a clientâ€™s
  investment goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon
- Design a structured **risk profiling questionnaire** to assess client risk appetite
- Construct a diversified investment portfolio using UK equities and British Government securities
- Analyse asset and portfolio performance using modern portfolio theory
- Evaluate risk and return using quantitative metrics such as:
  - Arithmetic and geometric mean returns
  - Volatility, covariance, and correlation
  - Beta and market risk exposure
  - Risk-adjusted performance measures (Sharpe Ratio, Treynor Measure)
- Compare portfolio performance against a UK market benchmark (FTSE Index)

## Assets & Data Sources

The portfolio and performance analysis are based on real historical market data obtained from
reliable financial data providers.

### Assets Analysed
- **UK Equities:** Two shares listed on the London Stock Exchange (LSE), selected from different
  industry sectors to enhance diversification
- **Market Benchmark:** FTSE Index (used to represent overall UK equity market performance)
- **Risk-Free Asset:** UK Government bond (Gilt) yields, used as a proxy for the risk-free rate

### Data Sources
- London Stock Exchange (LSE)
- FTSE Russell
- Yahoo Finance
- Bank of England (UK Government bond yields)
